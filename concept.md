# Wattplot — Concept Brief

A DIY, open-source 4x8 ft planter that grows a tomato crop on top while generating solar electricity underneath, in a mutually beneficial arrangement.

## Problem

Backyard, patio, and small-farm growers want to:
- Grow food in a controlled, raised bed
- Add solar generation without sacrificing growing space
- Avoid the cost and complexity of commercial agrivoltaic systems

Standard solutions force a trade-off: solar panel OR garden bed, not both.

## Core Idea

A 4x8 ft raised planter where:
- The **top surface is a tilted, semi-transparent solar canopy** acting as both PV array and partial shade / rain shelter for the crop
- The **bed below grows tomatoes** (and compatible companions) in soil
- The **symbiosis** is engineered: filtered light, reduced heat stress, less evaporation, rain diversion to roots — while electricity offsets pump, irrigation, lighting, or grid use

## Target User

- DIY maker / homesteader
- Smallholder farmer
- School / community garden
- Off-grid or resilience-minded household

Skill level: comfortable with basic carpentry and wiring. No specialized tools beyond a drill, saw, multimeter.

## Constraints (from the brief)

- 4 ft x 8 ft footprint
- Common / off-the-shelf materials
- Open-source (publishable drawings, BOM, code)
- Tomato as the primary crop

## Key Design Questions to Resolve

- Canopy tilt angle vs. sun-tracking vs. fixed
- Glazing: rigid panels, semi-transparent laminate, or bifacial modules with spacing
- Mounting height for crop clearance and airflow
- Water: rain capture into the bed, drip irrigation, soil-moisture sensing
- Electrical: grid-tie vs. off-grid battery vs. direct-load (pump, fan)
- Tomato variety: determinate bush vs. indeterminate vine (affects canopy height)
- Structural: wood frame, aluminum extrusion, or steel
- Thermal: does the canopy cook the bed? Ventilation gap?

## Out of Scope (initial version)

- Greenhouse automation
- Commercial-scale deployment
- Crops other than tomatoes (v1)
- Permitting / utility interconnection paperwork (v1)

## Success Looks Like

- One weekend build with under ~$500 in materials
- Produces 400-800 kWh/yr (estimate, to validate)
- 20-50 lb tomato harvest per season (estimate, to validate)
- Public repo with BOM, cut list, wiring diagram, firmware, planting guide
