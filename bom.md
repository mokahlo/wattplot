# Wattplot v1 — Bill of Materials

## Assumptions

These are baked into the quantities below. Flag anything you want to change.

- **Bed:** 4 ft × 8 ft outside dimensions, 11" soil depth (two 2×6 stacked)
- **Base:** 4×4 skids on grade (no concrete footings for v1)
- **Canopy tilt:** 35° from horizontal (good middle for ~30–45° latitudes)
- **Canopy overhang:** 0.4 ft past bed on high side + 0.4 ft on low side (20% total)
- **Joist count:** 5 joists at 24" OC (8 ft ridge ÷ 4 spaces = 24")
- **Joist length:** ~5.86 ft (cut from 8 ft stock, ~2.14 ft waste per board)
- **Wood:** pressure-treated southern yellow pine (or cedar for natural rot resistance, ~2× cost)
- **Fasteners:** exterior-rated (hot-dipped galvanized or ceramic-coated)
- **Panel mounting:** IronRidge XR-100 rails or equivalent, with mid- and end-clamps

## Hardware

### Bed assembly
- 3" exterior deck screws — ~3 lb box (~150 screws)
- 1/2" × 6" lag bolts, hot-dipped galv — 8 count (through 4×4 skids into 4×4 uprights)
- 1/2" washers — 8 count
- Optional: construction adhesive for bed walls (exterior-grade)

### Canopy-to-bed connection
- 1/2" × 6" lag bolts — 4 count (attach 4×4 uprights to bed corners)
- Joist hangers (2×4, galvanized) — 10 count (5 joists × 2 ends)
- OR hurricane ties Simpson H2.5A — 10 count (alternative to hangers)

### Solar panel mounting
- IronRidge XR-100 rail (or equivalent) — 4 × 4 ft sections, one per panel row
- Mid-clamps — 8 count (between panels)
- End-clamps — 8 count (panel edges)
- L-feet or roof mounts — 10 count (rail-to-joist attachment)
- 5/16" × 3" stainless lag bolts — 20 count (for L-feet)
- Grounding lugs — 4 count (one per panel)
- 10 AWG copper grounding wire — ~15 ft
